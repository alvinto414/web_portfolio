Interactive Earthquake Visualization

An interactive visualization on earthquakes occurred in US.

Completion Date:

April 14, 2014

Team Member:

  • Alvin To
  • Felix Lai

Responsibilities:

In this individual project, I was responsible for designing and programming a choropleth map and a graph that displays earthquake depths and magnitudes. This project was created with HTML and JavaScript with d3 library.

Outcome:

  • Able to efficiently program in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Able to transform text data into visualization

Introduction

We have chosen to create a Earthquake data visualization tool as our interactive visualization project. This project aims at providing analytical value and insight into the potential influence of earthquake activities’ in relationship to population density of cities across the United States of America. With various sets of earthquake data obtained from USGS and US population density data from US Census Bureau, we were able to geographically visualize earthquake activities from March 15 to 21 with “coordinates”, “depth”, “magnitude”, and “time” information on a US map with colour scaled population density.

Target Audience

The scope of this project could potentially be of interest to government leaders, officials, and related departments (eg. Department of Defense) concerned with disaster-relief, response, and management surrounding earthquakes. As a proof of concept to our project, the scope of visualization is currently limited to only within US region. However, the visualization could be potentially and easily be implemented for any regions, countries, continents in the world, as long as data is provided.

The Visualization

With our target audience in mind, we set out to think about the most crucial question about earthquakes that is most relevant to them: where are earthquakes happening that can potentially be very dangerous [high population density]. In order scope down relevancy of the information presented and provide insight, we have decided to provide various interactive tools, including timeline brushing, magnitude range slider, city network/ earthquake toggles, and tooltips. An accurate visualization of population and earthquake coordinates are presented with corresponding visual elements on a US map to provide contextual information. In essence, visual encoding variables such as position, colour, size is used to help create an effective visualization.
